Beef Lasagna

Description

This is a delicious beef lasagna recipe shamelessly stolen from Vincenzo Prosperi.

The original can be found here.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams veal mince
  • 500 grams pork mince
  • 1 carrot diced
  • 1 celery stick diced
  • 1/2 onion diced
  • 2 bottles Italian passata
  • 1 glass red wine
  • salt
  • pepper
  • provolone cheese
  • mozzarella cheese
  • grated pecorino or pamigiano reggiano
  • Lasagna pasta sheets
  • extra virgin olive oil
  • 25 grams butter
  • 25 grams all purpose flour
  • 250ml milk
  • sprinkle of salt
  • sprinkle of nutmeg

Steps

  1. Add a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil to a large pot and let it slowly heat.
  2. Add carrot, celery, and onion and cook until softened, 5-10 minutes.
  3. Add mincemeat to the pot and break it down using a wooden spoon. Simmer until it starts to brown, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add 1 1/2 bottles of passata and stir. Cover and simmer for one hour on medium low heat.
  5. Remove lid and add a glass of water. Let cook on low. Add more water if too dry.
  6. Cook uncovered for another hour, stirring occasionally
  7. Add butter to a small saucepan and put on low heat.
  8. Let melt slowly and stir in flour with a whisk. Whisk until fully incorporated.
  9. Slowly add milk, stirring continuously.
  10. Once creamy and thick, remove from heat and add salt and nutmeg.
  11. Brush baking dish with olive oil or melted butter.
  12. Evenly spread a scoop of meat sauce to the bottom of the dish
  13. Arrange pasta sheets in a single layer, cutting to size if need be.
  14. Spread another layer of beef sauce over the pasta. Sprinkle with pecorino and add provolone and mozzarella.
  15. Lightly spread some bechamel sauce.
  16. Add another layer of pasta sheets.
  17. Repeat until desired number of layers are created.
  18. Add plain basil tomato sauce on top, spreading over entire surface.
  19. Cover with foil and bake for 20 minutes at 350 degrees.
  20. Remove foil, generously add pecorino cheese, and bake uncovered for 10 minutes.
  21. Enjoy!
